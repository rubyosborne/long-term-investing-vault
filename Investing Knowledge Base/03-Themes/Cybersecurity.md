# Cybersecurity

**Status:** Watching
**Created:** 2026-05-19
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-19

## Description
The cybersecurity sector benefits from structural, non-discretionary spending growth driven by escalating threat landscapes, regulatory mandates, and the emerging quantum computing threat. Post-quantum cryptography migration creates a multi-decade upgrade cycle across every enterprise and government system worldwide.

## Key drivers
- Quantum computing threat: NIST has finalised post-quantum encryption standards. NSA mandates quantum-resistant systems by 2035.
- Regulatory tailwinds: SEC cyber disclosure rules (2024), EU DORA (2025), expanding state-level breach notification requirements.
- AI-powered threats increasing attack sophistication and frequency.
- Platform consolidation: enterprises moving from point solutions to integrated security platforms.
- Zero Trust architecture adoption accelerating across enterprises.
- Agentic AI infrastructure requiring new security paradigms for autonomous software agents.

- High valuations across the sector price in near-perfect execution
