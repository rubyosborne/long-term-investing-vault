## Section 7: New Ideas

### Pick 1: BRK.B (Berkshire Hathaway Class B)

**Conviction: MEDIUM**
**Bucket:** Growth compounder (defensive anchor)
**Theme:** Quality Value (new)
**Data confidence:** Full

**Bull thesis:** The most defensive mega-cap in the market at P/E 15x with $373B in cash, operating under new CEO Greg Abel who has explicitly committed to Buffett's capital allocation principles. Provides essential portfolio diversification away from AI Infrastructure concentration.

**Bear case (236 words):**
The Berkshire bull thesis rests on continuity, but continuity is the very thing at risk. Warren Buffett is 95 years old. While Abel has been groomed for years, the reality is nobody has tested whether Berkshire's unique owner-operator culture survives without its founder's personal relationships and reputation. Many of Berkshire's best deals (BNSF, the Goldman crisis preferreds, the Apple stake) came because Buffett's name opened doors that Abel's cannot.

The $373B cash pile is both a strength and a drag. In a rising-rate environment with 10Y at 4.45%, that cash earns meaningful income. But it also represents an implicit admission that Berkshire cannot find attractive deployment opportunities at current valuations. If rates decline, the cash yield disappears and the market will punish the drag.

Berkshire's P/E of 15x looks cheap, but the earnings mix includes insurance float income, equity portfolio gains, and operating businesses of wildly varying quality. The "real" earnings power is harder to pin down than a simple tech compounder. Apple, which represents a massive chunk of the equity portfolio, faces its own headwinds with China tariffs and AI product cycle uncertainty.

Finally, Berkshire's sheer size ($1T+ market cap) limits future returns. The law of large numbers means even excellent capital allocation generates diminishing percentage returns.

**Entry zone:** Buy zone within 5% of current (~$445-$469)

**Invalidation trigger:** Abel makes a material acquisition above $50B that destroys shareholder value (measured as >20% decline in book value per share within 12 months of deal close), or annual operating earnings decline >15% for 2 consecutive years absent recession.

**Dollar allocation proposal:** $75

### Pick 2: PLTR (Palantir Technologies)

**Conviction: LOW**
**Bucket:** Theme play (AI Software)
**Theme:** AI Infrastructure (existing)
**Data confidence:** Full (fresh Q1 data)

**Bull thesis:** The fastest-growing enterprise software company at scale. Revenue +85% YoY in Q1, US commercial +104%, government AI moat deepening with military AI contracts. Raised FY2026 revenue guidance to $7.65B (71% growth), crushing consensus by 5%.

**Bear case (248 words):**
Palantir's valuation defies gravity. At $145.50 and $0.33 EPS in Q1, the annualised trailing P/E sits around 110x. Even on the raised guidance of $7.65B revenue with 35% net margins (generous), that implies ~$2.68B net income on a $350B market cap, a forward P/E of ~130x. This is not a valuation that tolerates any disappointment.

The 85% revenue growth is stunning but partly reflects the Aleph platform's land-and-expand cycle reaching a temporary peak. Customer concentration remains high, with US government contracts subject to budget negotiations and political risk. A single cancelled contract (e.g. Project Maven 2.0) could remove hundreds of millions in ARR.

Stock-based compensation remains problematic. While improved from historic levels, SBC still dilutes shareholders and inflates apparent profitability on an adjusted basis. The company's GAAP operating margins are substantially lower than the adjusted figures management highlights.

The competitive moat is also less clear than bulls claim. Microsoft, AWS, and Google all offer competing AI/ML platforms with deeper enterprise relationships and lower switching costs for non-classified workloads. Palantir wins on US government clearance requirements, but commercial clients have alternatives.

Finally, at 110x+ earnings, a growth deceleration from 85% to even 50% could trigger a 30-40% drawdown. This happened in early 2026 when the stock fell 31% from its February peak.

**Entry zone:** Buy zone within 5% of current (~$138-$148)

**Invalidation trigger:** US commercial revenue growth decelerates below 50% YoY for 2 consecutive quarters, or total revenue growth falls below 40% YoY while P/E remains above 80x.

**Dollar allocation proposal:** $0 this week (watchlist only, see note below)

**Note:** Despite strong earnings, the valuation provides no margin of safety. Moving from "Flagged" to "Watching" on the watchlist. Will reconsider if price corrects to sub-$120 (P/E ~90x on current earnings run-rate) or if next quarter confirms sustained >80% growth trajectory.

### Pick 3: CELH (Celsius Holdings)

**Conviction: LOW**
**Bucket:** Theme play (Consumer Growth)
**Theme:** Consumer Growth (new)
**Data confidence:** Partial (pre-earnings, Q1 reports May 12)

**Bull thesis:** Down 65% from peak with fwd P/E ~23x for a brand growing revenue 129% (including Alani Nu acquisition). The acquisition transforms Celsius from a niche energy drink into a multi-brand platform competing with Monster and Red Bull at scale.

**Bear case (221 words):**
The 129% revenue growth headline is misleading. Organic growth (ex-Alani Nu) is likely in the 15-25% range, respectable but not exceptional for the multiple. The Alani Nu integration adds execution risk: combining distribution networks, rationalising SKUs, and managing brand identity for two distinct consumer bases (Celsius skews fitness/health, Alani Nu skews lifestyle/beauty).

Gross margins compressed to 47.4% from the mid-50s historically. Management targets "low 50s by mid-2026" but this guidance relies on cost synergies that may take longer than expected. Commodity costs (sweeteners, aluminium cans, shipping) are rising with inflation.

The competitive landscape is brutal. Monster and Red Bull control ~70% of global energy drink share and are investing heavily in functional/better-for-you sub-brands. PepsiCo's distribution agreement gives Celsius shelf space, but also creates dependency. If PepsiCo launches a competing brand (or acquires one), Celsius loses its distribution moat overnight.

Finally, the stock is cheap for a reason. Three consecutive quarters of missing Street expectations on margins drove the 65% drawdown. The market needs to see margin proof before re-rating, and that proof is still weeks away.

**Entry zone:** Buy zone within 5% of current (~$32-$35.50)

**Invalidation trigger:** Organic revenue (ex-acquisition) declines YoY for any quarter, or gross margins fail to recover above 50% by Q3 2026.

**Dollar allocation proposal:** $0 this week (watchlist, pending May 12 earnings)

**Bear case (209 words):**
CrowdStrike has never reported positive GAAP earnings. The company relies on adjusted metrics that exclude substantial stock-based compensation. At ~$450 per share with no GAAP profitability, investors are paying for a future that may not arrive quickly enough.

The July 2024 global outage left lasting scars. While ARR has recovered, the incident created an opening for Microsoft Defender (bundled with E5 licenses) and Palo Alto Networks (Cortex XDR). Enterprise security procurement teams now ask about redundancy and concentration risk, questions that favour multi-vendor approaches over Falcon's consolidation pitch.

The cybersecurity market is growing at 12-15% annually, but CrowdStrike needs to grow at 25%+ to justify its valuation. This requires both new customer acquisition and module expansion. If net retention rates (currently ~115%) decline below 110%, the growth flywheel stalls.

Valuation at ~$450 implies approximately 20x forward revenue, one of the most expensive multiples in enterprise software. A growth disappointment or broader tech multiple compression could easily drive a 20-30% correction. The stock is already down from its November 2025 high of $567.

**Entry zone:** Buy zone within 5% of current (~$428-$473)

**Invalidation trigger:** ARR growth decelerates below 20% YoY for 2 consecutive quarters, or a material security incident affecting Falcon platform integrity.
