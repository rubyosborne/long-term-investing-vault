### Pick 3: NVDA (NVIDIA) - LOW Conviction

**Conviction:** LOW
**Bucket:** Growth compounder (AI Infrastructure)
**Data confidence:** Partial (pre-earnings, May 20 report pending)

**Bull thesis:** Dominant AI GPU supplier with 73% revenue growth, benefiting directly from $660B+ hyperscaler capex cycle, with CUDA ecosystem creating durable competitive moat that custom silicon cannot easily replicate.

**Bear case (410 words):**
At $5.46T market cap and ~42x trailing P/E, NVDA is the most expensive large-cap stock in the world by market value. The price embeds years of sustained hypergrowth that must materialise perfectly. Any deceleration will compress the multiple violently.

The custom silicon risk is not hypothetical. It is happening now. Google TPUs already handle a significant and growing portion of Google's inference workloads. Google Cloud offers TPU v5p to external customers, directly competing with NVIDIA GPUs in the cloud. Amazon's Trainium 2 chips are in production and are being deployed across AWS for both training and inference. Microsoft's Maia 100 accelerator is ramping for Azure workloads. Meta has its own MTIA chips. Combined, these four hyperscalers represent NVIDIA's largest customers and they are all actively reducing their dependency on NVIDIA silicon.

If custom silicon captures 20-30% of inference workloads over the next 2-3 years (training is harder to displace due to CUDA lock-in), NVIDIA's total addressable market contracts by $50-80B at current run rates. Inference is the faster-growing segment, so losing share there is worse than losing share in training.

Revenue growth is decelerating. From 262% to 122% to 73%, and each step down gets punished in the multiple. The market prices NVIDIA as a software-like compounder, but it is fundamentally a semiconductor company subject to cyclical demand patterns. The $660B hyperscaler capex cycle will peak. Every capex cycle does. When it does, NVIDIA's revenue could stall or decline, just as it did in 2022-2023. The market has a short memory for semiconductor cyclicality.

Geopolitical risk from China export controls limits the addressable market by an estimated $10-15B annually. Each tightening of restrictions (and the current administration has been unpredictable on trade policy) could further constrain revenue.

The May 20 earnings report is a binary event. Options market implies an ~8% move. Buying a $75 position 8 days before a binary event at a 42x P/E is not prudent capital allocation for a long-term investor. The risk/reward is better assessed post-earnings, when the growth trajectory for FY27 becomes clearer. Recommending a small $75 position this week is aggressive given the timing.

If earnings disappoint even modestly (missing by 2-3%), the stock could drop 15-20% in a day. There is no valuation floor at these levels. Patience is a legitimate strategy.

**Entry zone:** Buy zone within 5% of current (~$210-$222), but strongly prefer to wait for May 20 earnings.
**Invalidation trigger:** Data centre revenue growth falls below 40% YoY for any quarter, or combined hyperscaler capex guidance is cut by >10%.

**Dollar allocation:** $75
