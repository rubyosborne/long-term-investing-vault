**Bear case (213 words):**
NVIDIA trades at 30x+ trailing earnings, pricing in continued hypergrowth that may not materialise. The transition from Blackwell to Vera Rubin creates execution risk: customers may delay orders waiting for next-gen silicon, creating an air pocket in revenue. Hyperscalers are aggressively developing custom chips. Google's TPU v6, Amazon's Trainium3, and Microsoft's Maia 2 all directly compete for AI training and inference workloads. If custom silicon captures even 30% of hyperscaler compute spend by 2028, NVIDIA's datacenter pricing power erodes materially. Export controls remain a persistent threat: the US government has repeatedly tightened chip export restrictions to China, which historically represented 20%+ of NVIDIA's revenue. Further restrictions could shave billions from the top line. The $79B expected quarterly revenue implies an annual run rate of $316B+. At some point, the law of large numbers bites. Finally, if the AI capex cycle peaks before NVIDIA's revenue does, multiple compression could be severe. A rerating from 30x to 20x earnings, even with flat revenue, implies a 33% drawdown from current levels. The stock has already run 500%+ from its 2022 lows, meaning a lot of good news is priced in.

The cybersecurity market is cyclical in practice. Budget cuts during economic slowdowns disproportionately hit security vendors who are perceived as premium-priced additions to existing Microsoft Defender capabilities. At $562.50, the stock prices in near-perfect execution. Any stumble in Charlotte AI monetisation, a second operational incident, or a meaningful loss of share to Microsoft's bundled security offering would be severely punished by the market.
