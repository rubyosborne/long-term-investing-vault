## Section 7: New Ideas

### Pick 1: GOOGL (Alphabet) - Growth Compounder

**Conviction: HIGH**
**Bucket:** Growth compounder
**Data confidence:** Full

**Bull thesis:** Alphabet delivered a blowout Q1 with Cloud revenue up 63% and Cloud margins expanding from 17.8% to 32.9%, proving that AI infrastructure spend is translating to profitability. At 32x trailing earnings with 22% revenue growth and 81% net income growth, this is a quality compounder at a reasonable price.

**Bear case:**
Google's dominance in search is under genuine threat from AI-native interfaces. ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude all offer answer-first experiences that bypass traditional search. If even 10-15% of search queries migrate to AI alternatives over the next 3 years, that represents $30-40B in at-risk advertising revenue. The company is spending aggressively on AI ($175-185B capex in 2026), but the return profile on this investment is unproven at this scale. Cloud growth at 63% is extraordinary, but this partly reflects catch-up from a lower base. AWS and Azure have deeper enterprise relationships. The 34% April rally prices in a lot of the good news from Q1, and any deceleration in Q2 could trigger a sharp rerating given the stock is at its 52-week high. Regulatory risk remains material: the DOJ antitrust case over search distribution could force structural remedies including potential separation of Chrome or Android. The European Digital Markets Act imposes ongoing compliance costs. Alphabet's Other Bets continue to consume capital ($1.5B+ quarterly losses) with no clear timeline to profitability. At 32x earnings, the stock is no longer cheap relative to its own history, and multiple contraction in a rising rate environment is a real risk.

**Entry zone:** Buy zone within 5% of current (~$366-$386)
**Invalidation trigger:** Cloud growth decelerates below 30% for 2 consecutive quarters while margins compress below 25%. Or: search revenue declines year-over-year for any quarter.

### Pick 2: MSFT (Microsoft) - Growth Compounder

**Conviction: HIGH**
**Bucket:** Growth compounder
**Data confidence:** Full

**Bull thesis:** Azure grew 40% in Q3 FY26, validating the AI infrastructure thesis. At 25x trailing P/E (19% below the 10-year median of 33x), Microsoft is the cheapest it has been relative to its own history in years, with the deepest enterprise distribution moat in tech.

**Bear case:**
Microsoft's Azure growth at 40% looks impressive, but the incremental capital required to sustain it is enormous. The company guided $190B in capex for the year, and at some point investors will ask whether these returns justify the spend. The end of the OpenAI revenue-sharing arrangement, while positive for margins, signals a relationship that is evolving in unpredictable ways. Microsoft bet heavily on OpenAI, and any further distancing could undermine their AI differentiation. The PC market remains structurally flat, and Office 365 price increases face pushback from enterprise customers dealing with their own cost pressures. Windows Copilot monetisation has been slower than expected, with enterprise adoption of AI features still in early innings. Competition in cloud is intensifying: Google Cloud grew 63% last quarter, nearly double Azure's rate, suggesting Microsoft's lead is narrowing in key segments. The gaming division (Activision Blizzard) has yet to deliver the synergies promised at acquisition. At 25x earnings, MSFT appears cheap relative to its median, but this discount may reflect legitimate concerns about capital allocation discipline and the sustainability of AI-driven growth once the initial infrastructure buildout peaks.

**Entry zone:** Buy zone within 5% of current (~$397-$418)
**Invalidation trigger:** Azure growth decelerates below 25% for 2 consecutive quarters, or total revenue growth falls below 10% YoY.

### Pick 3: AMZN (Amazon) - Growth Compounder

**Conviction: MEDIUM**
**Bucket:** Growth compounder
**Data confidence:** Full

**Bull thesis:** AWS re-accelerated to 28% growth (15-quarter high) while operating margins hit a record 13.1%. Amazon is building the dominant cloud and AI compute platform with a retail business that now generates real margin. The Anthropic partnership ($100B AWS commitment) locks in a major AI workload.

**Bear case:**
Amazon's record operating margins mask a fundamental tension: the company is simultaneously trying to expand profitability while committing $200B in capital expenditure for 2026, the largest annual spend of any company in history. Free cash flow collapsed from $25.9B to $1.2B year-over-year in Q1, and this compression could persist for multiple quarters as infrastructure buildout continues. AWS re-acceleration to 28% is encouraging, but Google Cloud at 63% growth is gaining share fast, and custom silicon could commoditise the compute layer over time. The retail business faces margin pressure from Temu and Shein, which have captured meaningful share in price-sensitive segments. Prime membership growth is decelerating in mature markets, and same-day delivery costs are rising. Amazon's advertising business faces increasing regulatory scrutiny and competition from retail media networks at Walmart and others. The stock trading at its all-time high of $264 leaves no room for execution misses. If Q2 results show any deceleration or margin compression, the stock could retrace 15-20% quickly. The Anthropic $100B commitment ties significant resources to a single AI partner whose competitive position remains uncertain.

**Entry zone:** Buy zone within 5% of current (~$250-$264)
**Invalidation trigger:** AWS growth decelerates below 20% for 2 consecutive quarters, or operating margins fall below 10%.
**Dollar allocation:** NZD $50
